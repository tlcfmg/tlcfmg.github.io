## AI Detective

Most teachers are convinced they can tell AI-generated text from human writing. AI Detective puts that confidence to the test. The app presents research paper abstracts one at a time and asks you to judge each one: human or AI? After each answer, you see immediately whether you were right — and why.

The results are often surprising. Instructors who feel certain they can detect AI reliably tend to perform closer to chance than they expect. That gap between perceived and actual accuracy is the starting point for a much more productive conversation about AI in academic writing.

Developed by TLC-FMG at the University of Amsterdam. Currently seeded with papers from communication science.

### Key Features

- **Test your own detection skills** read each abstract and judge: human or AI-generated?
- **Immediate feedback** see the correct answer with an explanation after each attempt
- **Score tracking** your running score updates with each round so you can see how well you actually do
- **Classroom-ready** works as a whole-class projection activity or an individual self-test
- **Open source** source code freely available on [GitHub](https://github.com/tlcfmg/aidetective)

### Suitable For

- Staff development sessions on AI and academic integrity
- Instructors who want to calibrate their own expectations before setting AI policy
- Research methods and academic writing courses
- AI literacy workshops
